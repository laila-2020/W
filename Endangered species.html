Giant panda

Pandas live in the bamboo forests of China and spend most of their time eating bamboo. They are a symbol of wildlife conservation. Their main threat is habitat loss, as forests are cleared for farming and development, leaving them with less food and space.

Bluewhale

Blue whales, the largest creatures to ever live, feed mainly on tiny krill. They are important for ocean ecosystems by supporting the food chain. Once heavily hunted for oil, they remain endangered today from ship strikes, noise pollution, and climate change.

Rhinoceros

Rhinos are strong herbivores with thick skin and horns used for defense. They help shape their ecosystems by grazing. Sadly, they are critically endangered due to poaching for their horns and habitat loss caused by human settlement.

Orangutan

Orangutans are highly intelligent apes found in the rainforests of Borneo and Sumatra. They build nests in trees and share 97% of their DNA with humans. They are endangered due to massive deforestation for palm oil plantations and illegal hunting.

Gorilla

Gorillas are gentle giants living in African forests, feeding on plants, fruits, and insects. They are very social and share close DNA with humans. They are endangered from poaching, diseases like Ebola, and the destruction of their forest homes.

Macaw parrot

Macaws are brightly colored parrots native to Central and South American rainforests. Known for their intelligence and loud calls, they are endangered due to illegal pet trade, hunting, and rapid loss of rainforest habitats.

Monarch butterfly

Monarch butterflies are famous for their long migration from North America to Mexico. They pollinate plants and are vital to ecosystems. Their numbers are shrinking due to climate change, pesticides, and the loss of milkweed plants they rely on for survival.
