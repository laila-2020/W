Forest

Forests are habitats filled with tall trees, plants, and rich wildlife. They provide shelter and food for many animals such as tigers, monkeys, owls, and snakes. Forests are vital for clean air and balance in nature.

Grassland

Grasslands are wide open areas covered with tall grasses and few trees. They are home to animals like lions, zebras, elephants, and giraffes. These habitats support large herds and many predators.

Polar regions ( Arctic & Antaractic )

Polar regions are cold, icy habitats found at the Earth’s poles. Animals like polar bears, penguins, seals, and whales survive in these extreme conditions. They are covered with snow and ice most of the year.

Deserts

Deserts are hot, dry habitats with little rain and few plants. Animals such as camels, lizards, foxes, and scorpions have special adaptations to survive here. Despite the harsh climate, deserts are full of unique life.

Ocean and seas

Oceans and seas are vast salty waters that cover most of the Earth. They are home to dolphins, whales, sharks, turtles, and countless fish. These habitats are essential for life on our planet.

Wetlands

Wetlands are areas of land covered with shallow water, such as rivers, lakes, and marshes. They provide a home for crocodiles, hippos, frogs, and many fish. Wetlands are important for clean water and protecting wildlife.

Mountains

Mountains are high habitats with cold air and rocky slopes. Animals like snow leopards, mountain goats, and eagles live here. They adapt to steep land and harsh weather conditions.

Taiga (Boreal Forest)

The taiga is a cold forest made of pine, spruce, and fir trees. Animals such as bears, wolves, moose, and lynxes live here. It is the world’s largest land habitat, stretching across northern regions.

Rainforest

Rainforests are warm, wet habitats with thick trees and plants. They are home to jaguars, parrots, frogs, and monkeys. Rainforests are full of life and provide much of Earth’s oxygen.

Svannah woodlands

Savannah woodlands are open areas with scattered trees and tall grasses. Animals like elephants, giraffes, antelopes, and lions live here. These habitats have both wet and dry seasons that shape the wildlife.

Volcanic islands

Volcanic islands are formed by eruptions and surrounded by ocean waters. They host unique animals like iguanas, crabs, seabirds, and rare plants. Many species here are found nowhere else.

Caves

Caves are dark, sheltered habitats found underground or in mountains. Animals such as bats, owls, insects, and blind fish live here. They provide safe homes for creatures that need cool, quiet places.
